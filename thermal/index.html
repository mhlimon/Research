Thermal Properties

Characterization of thermal and mechanical properties of stanene nanoribbons: a molecular dynamics study

  • Author: Asir, Ratul, Samia
  • Material:
    • Stanene - buckled honeycomb structure of Tin
  • Size:
    • Nanoribbon;  10 nm × 3 nm zigzag and armchair
    • height (0.88 Å) 
  • Process:
    • EMD
    • DFT
  • Tools/Softwares:
    • LAMMPS
    • Quantum Espresso
  • Variable Characteristics:
    • Temperature, Width,
    • Defect - point vacancy, edge vacancy and bi-vacancy
    • Stress - strain
  • Potentials:
    • MEAM
  • Result:
    • TC --> 0.95 ± 0.024 W m−1 K−1 and 0.89 ± 0.026 W m−1 K−1
    • electron effective mass for pristine and defected stanene are ∼0.49 × 106 ms−1 and ∼0.2 × 106 ms−1 
  • Key Foundings:
    • TC decrease with Temperature increase
    • TC increase with Width increase
    • Applicability in Thermoelectrics: small electron effective mass and high fermi velocity

  • Material:
