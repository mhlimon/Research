Thermal Properties

Characterization of thermal and mechanical properties of stanene nanoribbons: a molecular dynamics study

  • Author: Asir, Ratul, Samia
  • Material:
    • Stanene - buckled honeycomb structure of Tin
  • Details of Structure: 
    • Size-Nanoribbon;  10 nm × 3 nm zigzag and armchair
    • height (0.88 Å) 
  • Process:
    • EMD
    • DFT
  • Tools/Softwares:
    • LAMMPS
    • Quantum Espresso
  • Variable Characteristics:
    • Temperature, Width,
    • Defect - point vacancy, edge vacancy and bi-vacancy
    • Stress - strain
  • Potentials:
    • MEAM
  • Result:
    • TC --> 0.95 ± 0.024 W m−1 K−1 and 0.89 ± 0.026 W m−1 K−1
    • electron effective mass for pristine and defected stanene are ∼0.49 × 106 ms−1 and ∼0.2 × 106 ms−1 
  • Key Foundings:
    • TC decrease with Temperature increase
    • TC increase with Width increase
    • Applicability in Thermoelectrics: small electron effective mass and high fermi velocity

Thermal transport characterization of stanene/silicene heterobilayer and stanene bilayer nanostructures

  • Author: Maliha Noshin, Asir Intisar Khan and Samia Subrina

  • Material:

    • Stanene/Silicene- heterobilayer
    • Stanene bilayer

  • Details of Structure: 

    • Nanoribbon;  10 nm × 3 nm
    • Height (0.88 Å) 
    • Lattice constant:
      • stanene: 4.72 Å
      • silicene: 3.8Å
    • Bond length:
      • Sn-Sn : 2.84Å
      • Si-Si : 2.24Å
    • Buckling Height
      • Si : 0.43Å
      • Sn : 0.83 Å
    • Interlayer distance: 3.4 Å
    • Supercell composition of 5x5 lateral periodicity of silicene and 4x4 lateral periodicity of stanene.

  • Process:

    • EMD
    • PDOS
  • Tools/Softwares:
    • LAMMPS
    • Phana

  • Variable Characteristics:

    • Temperature, Width,

  • Potentials:

    • Sn-Sn: Optimized Tersoff; Parameters:
    • Si-Si: Stillinger-Weber (SW)
    • For Heterobilayer: LJ- Ɛ(𝑆𝑛−𝑆𝑖) = 20.703 𝑚𝑒𝑉, 𝜎(𝑆𝑛−𝑆𝑖) = 4.1 Å and 𝑟𝑐 = 3.5 𝜎(𝑆𝑛−𝑆𝑖)

-Simulation Parameters: - Volume: In the case of the silicene/stanene heterobilayer, the van der Waals thickness of the bilayer is taken to be 8.7 Å, which is the summation of the thickness of silicene layer28(4.2 Å) and stanene monolayer (4.5 Å including its buckling height). On the other hand, for stanene bilayer, the van der Waals thickness is taken to be 9 Å. Thickness Choice Ref1,Ref2,Ref3 - Periodic Boundary: Zigzag direction - Ts : 0.5 fs - 3 x 10^5 Timesteps - Nose-Hoover thermostat - 10^5 Timesteps - NVE ensemble - Stepest decent algorithm

  • Result:

    • TC --> stanene/silicene heterobilayer nanoribbon at room temperature (300 K) is 3.63± 0.27 W/m-K and 1.31 ± 0.34 W/m-K for stanene bilayer

  • Key Foundings:

    • TC decrease with Temperature increase
    • TC increase with Width increase
    • Applicability in Thermoelectrics: small electron effective mass and high fermi velocity
